Literature Review Outline

Introduction –

It is vital to leverage modern information and communication technologies for teaching and learning in higher education institutions. This study conducts a literature review and establishes a scholarly background by exploring some of the contributions made to the notion of e-learning, specifically its application in teaching and learning in higher educational institutions by various academics and institutions. It displays some of the worldwide perspectives expressed by individuals and institutions through surveys and other observations.

It investigates the numerous definitions of e-learning offered by scholars, the role of e-learning in higher educational institutions’ teaching and learning processes, and the benefits and drawbacks of its adoption and implementation.

Use of Technology –

The e-learning system has earned a competitive advantage over the prevalent traditional method. When it comes to education, e-learning is taking over. As a result, the eLearning teaching-learning approach plays a crucial part in the education system since it provides greater flexibility and allows independence from time, place, physical presence, and hectic and stressful teaching-learning. However, several issues must be overcome for E-Learning to succeed as a teaching-learning approach (Quadri et al., 2017).

An integrated information technology system must be established and developed before e-learning may be adopted. Several essential success elements for e-learning may help universities and instructors use e-learning technology more successfully and efficiently (Tawafak et al., 2019).

Security Consideration –

Organisations need to be aware of the security concerns of e-learning because more and more people are taking online courses. Companies that utilise e-learning platforms to train their personnel must pay special attention to security issues. Learning over the Internet is becoming increasingly common, and the Internet has become a new forum for various unlawful activities; therefore, e-learning environments are vulnerable to these risks. This paper will examine some of the most common dangers of online education and some potential solutions. This paper will also discuss the most popular open-source e-learning systems’ security and privacy flaws (Costinela-Luminiţa and Nicoleta-Magdalena, 2012).

Privacy & legislation in E-Learning –

Universities and other organisations are turning to e-learning to deliver training online for various reasons. However, the requirements for privacy and security have always been largely overlooked. The “Privacy Principles” are used to examine the privacy needs of e-learning systems. The capabilities of a variety of existing privacy-enhancing technologies are studied and assessed, including approaches for network privacy, policy-based privacy/security management, and trust systems (May, Fessakis, Dimitracopoulou and George, 2012).

Advantages – benefits of e-learning –

The adoption of e-learning in education, particularly in higher educational institutions, has many advantages, and as a result of these advantages, e-learning is regarded one of the best modes of education. Numerous research authors have discussed the pros and cons of integrating e-learning tools into schools (Arkorful & Abaidoo, 2022).

Disadvantages of e-learning –

Despite the benefits of e-learning when used in education, it does have some drawbacks. Several studies have identified drawbacks to online education (Collins et al. 2002). E-learning may also result in website congestion or excessive use, resulting in unanticipated time and financial implications (Arkorful & Abaidoo, 2022).

Conclusion –

E-learning is the process of teaching and learning through digital tools. It uses technological tools to enable learners to study conveniently and from any location. However, technological challenges need addressing, such as security and privacy.

A supportive environment for student collaboration can help raise academic standards. The body of literature describing the benefits and drawbacks of e-learning indicates the importance of its application in higher education for teachers, administrators, and students to reap the full benefits of its acceptance and implementation.
